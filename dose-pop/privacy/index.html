Dose Pop Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

Dose Pop is a simple medication reminder app for iPhone and iPad. This policy explains how the app handles information.

Data we collect

None. Dose Pop does not collect, transmit, sell, rent, or share personal data.

Data stored on your device

The app stores your medication list, dose history, notification preferences, and related app settings locally on your device using Apple frameworks. This information stays on your device.

iCloud sync

If iCloud sync is enabled by Apple system settings, your app data may sync through your own iCloud account. The developer does not have access to your iCloud data.

Medication search

When you search for a medication name, Dose Pop may make anonymous, read-only requests to public medication databases such as RxNorm and OpenFDA. These requests contain the search text you type. Dose Pop does not attach an account, advertising identifier, or tracking identifier to those requests.

Notifications

Dose Pop uses Apple local notifications to remind you when it is time to take a medication. Notification content is scheduled and shown by your device. It does not pass through a Dose Pop server.

Caregiver alerts

Caregiver cloud alerts are not active in this local-first version. If a future version adds caregiver alerts or cloud sharing, this policy and the App Store privacy answers will be updated before that feature is released.

Children

Dose Pop is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from any user.

Medical disclaimer

Dose Pop is a reminder tool. It does not diagnose, treat, prescribe, or provide medical advice. Always follow instructions from your doctor, pharmacist, or other qualified healthcare professional.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change. The current version will remain available at this URL.
